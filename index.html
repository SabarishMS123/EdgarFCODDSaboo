The Foundation of Relational Database Management

Edgar F. Codd

The Father of Relational Database Theory

Edgar Frank "Ted" Codd (1923-2003) was a British computer scientist who, while working for IBM, invented the relational model for database management, the theoretical basis for relational databases and relational database management systems.


In 1985, Codd published his famous 12 rules to define what constitutes a truly relational database system. These rules serve as a benchmark for evaluating how well a database management system adheres to the relational model.


His work revolutionized how we store, organize, and retrieve data, forming the foundation of modern database systems used across the globe today.
